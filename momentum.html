Understanding The Problem

Project Brief
The Problem

"It's hard to keep up an exercise routine!"

Users need an app that helps them to find a personalized exercise and me-time routine, which they actually can keep up. They finally want to create a healthy habit that stays in their life. Having group classes and access to a community of like-minded people will help the user with their personal motivation. Users are concerned about the possibility of injury so a personal coach will be available to provide a personalized routine.

Questions I've asked myselfs

  • Can an AI powered personal trainer fulfill customers expectations?

  • How important is community for an user? Is it necessary to include it?

  • What motivates the user to exercise, what is holding them back?

Interviews

Because I want to build an app specifically for people which are having a hard time building an exercise routine, I decided to interview those which are already successfully in doing exactly that!

Everyone has pain points even people which are successful in keeping up their routine.

“My Personal Trainer is like a friend!“

Key Learnings:

  • -In person Personal Trainer is the key to longterm success for many

  • Community & working-out together increases endurance & motivation

  • The Safety is a concern, which is holding people back from exercising

Execution

Low-Fidelity Prototype

How do I manifest what I've learned?

Here I pretty much fused together all the results of the research with the ideas that I had along the way. Inputs from competitive research but also inspirations through the interviews shine through the momentary results.

Mid-Fidelity Protoype

What do I know already?

I went straight into to the mid-fidelity wireframeing because the low-fidelity process made me question more and gave me already a deeper understanding of how to solve smaller problems. At this point, I started to question decisions that I made up to this point. I included notes and additional ideas to keep track of the progress but also to be clear which questions I need clarifications for.

In-Person User Testing

Participants seemed mostly very satisfied and expressed ease of use. However during the test some errors and observations were made which needed to be addressed and improved.

Testing Results

„What does the my activity section represent?“

5 out of 6 participants weren't sure what the my activity section represents. Here I actually had to go back and do some more competitor research since I didn't gain enough clarity what information the user needs and wants.

Preference Testing

After all the discoveries and changes I focused on the UI design. Users opinions were here also a key for success.

Final Prototype

Key Learnings:

I've learned specifically to set goals and intentions behind user testing, user research and basically just behind everything that goes into this project. At one point I did a card sort but I wasn't really clear on questions I wanted to have answered with it. The participants mirrored the unsure intentions right back at me.
